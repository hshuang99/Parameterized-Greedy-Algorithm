\label{ch:experimental-results}

All experiments were conducted on a system equipped with an AMD Ryzen™ 9 7940HS processor, running Ubuntu 24.04.4 LTS as the guest operating system, with 32 GB of RAM.

\subsection{Compounding Effect Across Rounds}
\label{subsec:compounding-effect}

The per-layer improvement reported in Table~\ref{tab:mixnibbles-comparison} applies to a
single MixNibbles invocation. Since MixNibbles is executed once per round, and KLEIN-64/64,
KLEIN-64/80, and KLEIN-64/96 respectively perform 12, 16, and 20 rounds, the CNOT and depth
savings accumulate linearly with the round count. Table~\ref{tab:mixnibbles-full-cipher}
reports the aggregated MixNibbles-only cost across all rounds for each KLEIN variant.

Because MixNibbles is applied identically in every round and the per-round CNOT and depth
figures are constant, the percentage reduction (\SI{64.4}{\percent} CNOT, \SI{94.2}{\percent}
depth) is invariant with respect to the number of rounds; only the absolute magnitude of the
savings grows with the cipher variant. For KLEIN-64/96, the proposed circuit eliminates
\num{8040} CNOT gates and \num{2940} layers of depth attributable to MixNibbles alone,
relative to the implementation of Mukherjee et al.~\cite{cryptoeprint:2026/1007}.

\paragraph{Impact on the full-cipher totals.}
Since MixNibbles constitutes one of four components combined in each round (AddRoundKey,
SubNibbles, MixNibbles, and the key scheduler), substituting the proposed MixNibbles circuit
into the full-round gate budget of \cite{cryptoeprint:2026/1007} allows the total circuit CNOT
count to be recomputed directly. Table~\ref{tab:full-cipher-cnot} reports the resulting
full-cipher CNOT totals, obtained by replacing the 624-CNOT MixNibbles contribution in each
round with the proposed 222-CNOT implementation while leaving AddRoundKey, SubNibbles, and
the key scheduler unchanged.

\midrule

\end{tabular}

\caption{Full-cipher CNOT count with the proposed MixNibbles circuit substituted into the round structure of \cite{cryptoeprint:2026/1007}.}
\label{tab:full-cipher-cnot}
\end{table}

The original totals in Table~\ref{tab:full-cipher-cnot} match those reported in
Table~6 of \cite{cryptoeprint:2026/1007}, confirming that the recomputation is consistent with
their stated methodology. Despite MixNibbles accounting for only one of four components in
the round function, its dominant share of the per-round CNOT budget (\SI{72.6}{\percent} of
the round total in the original design, $624/860$) means that optimizing this single layer
alone is sufficient to reduce the overall cipher circuit's CNOT count by approximately
\SI{46}{\percent} across all three KLEIN variants.

\paragraph{A note on depth compounding.}
Unlike CNOT count, circuit depth does not simply sum across independent components within a
single round, since gates from different components may be scheduled concurrently where data
dependencies allow. However, because each round of KLEIN depends on the output of the
previous round (AddRoundKey of round $i+1$ consumes the MixNibbles output of round $i$),
successive rounds cannot be parallelized with one another, and the per-round critical-path
depth accumulates additively across rounds regardless of intra-round scheduling. The
\SI{94.2}{\percent} depth reduction of the proposed MixNibbles circuit therefore translates
directly into a reduction of the same magnitude in the portion of the critical path
attributable to MixNibbles across the full cipher, as shown in
Table~\ref{tab:mixnibbles-full-cipher}. Establishing the precise reduction in total
circuit depth, accounting for the interleaving of MixNibbles with AddRoundKey, SubNibbles,
and the key scheduler within each round, requires a full round-level circuit scheduling
analysis and is left as a direction for future work.
